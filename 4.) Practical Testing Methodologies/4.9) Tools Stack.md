## Tools Stack: gau, waybackurls, ffuf, nuclei, httpx, subfinder

- What it does: Fetches known URLs of a domain from sources like Wayback Machine, Common Crawl, AlienVault's OTX,
                URLScan, etc.
- Why it's useful:
        Reveals legacy paths, hidden endpoints, deleted resources that may still be accessible.
        Useful for testing parameters on outdated APIs.
    Example:

       echo "target.com" | gau > all_urls.txt  


#### ðŸŒ³ Waybackurls:-
- What it does: Similar to gau, but focuses on extracting URLs from the Internet Archiveâ€™s Wayback Machine.
- Why it's useful: Easy to pipe into tools like gf or qsreplace for pattern-based testing. Example:

      echo "target.com" | waybackurls > legacy.txt  

Tip: Combine gau and waybackurls, then deduplicate:

    cat gau.txt waybackurls.txt | sort -u > merged_urls.txt


#### ðŸŒ³ ffuf (Fuzz Faster U Fool):- 
- What it does: A fast web fuzzer for directory brute-force, parameter discovery, and more.
- Why it's useful:

      Bruteforces directories or filenames using custom wordlists
      Supports GET and POST requests
      Can be used for VHOST fuzzing and recursive discovery
  
    Example:

      ffuf -u https://target.com/FUZZ -w /path/to/wordlist.txt -mc all  

    Advanced:

      ffuf -w wordlist.txt -u https://target.com/page.php?file=FUZZ  

#### ðŸŒ³ Nuclei:-
- What it does: Vulnerability scanner using YAML-based templates.
- Why it's useful:

      Detects known CVEs, misconfigurations, and exposure with speed and precision
      Highly customizable

Example:

    nuclei -u https://target.com -t vulnerabilities/  

- Best used with updated template repo:

       nuclei -update-templates

- Filter by severity:

      nuclei -l urls.txt -severity high,critical  

 
#### ðŸŒ³ HTTPX:-
- What it does: Probes a list of URLs and gathers metadata (status code, title, technologies, redirects, etc.)
- Why it's useful:

      Quickly filters out live hosts
      Helps identify interesting responses

Example:

      cat subdomains.txt | httpx -status-code -title -tech-detect  

You can use it with `-sc`, `-follow-redirects`, or `-web-server `for extra detail.


#### ðŸŒ³ Subfinder:-
- What it does: Finds subdomains using passive sources (Shodan, CertSpotter, AlienVault, etc.)
- Why it's useful:

       Core of passive recon and surface expansion
       Easily automated in scripts

Example:

    subfinder -d target.com -o subdomains.txt  

Tip: Combine with `httpx` to check which subdomains are live:

     subfinder -d target.com | httpx -silent
